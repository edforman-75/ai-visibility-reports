Methodology

How this AI visibility report was generated and what the metrics mean.

How We Test AI Visibility

We submit consumer questions to AI surfaces exactly as a real consumer would - through web browsers and chat interfaces. We then analyze responses for brand mentions, positioning, and accuracy.

Testing Process

  1. Question Development: Create questions based on real consumer search behavior and product categories
  2. Surface Testing: Submit each question to multiple AI surfaces
  3. Response Capture: Record full responses including brand mentions and positioning
  4. Analysis: Identify brand mentions, competitive mentions, and response quality
  5. Scoring: Calculate visibility rates and track changes over time

Why Browser Testing Matters

AI services often provide different responses through their web interfaces vs. API calls. Browser testing captures what consumers actually see when they search.

Understanding AI Response Differences

The same AI model can give different answers depending on how it's accessed. Browser interfaces often include additional context, personalization, and web search capabilities that affect product recommendations.

Metrics Explained

Visibility Rate

Formula: (Number of queries where brand was mentioned) / (Number of relevant queries) Ã— 100

This measures how often a brand appears when consumers ask relevant questions. A 42% visibility rate means the brand was mentioned in 42% of applicable queries.

Visibility Ratings

Rating Visibility Rate Meaning
Excellent 35%+ Brand appears frequently; strong AI presence
Good 15-34% Brand appears regularly; solid presence
Moderate 5-14% Brand appears sometimes; room for improvement
Critical 0-4% Brand rarely/never appears; urgent attention needed

Success Rate

Percentage of queries that received valid responses. Some surfaces may block automated testing or return errors.
